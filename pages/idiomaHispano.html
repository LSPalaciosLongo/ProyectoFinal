THE SPANISH LANGUAGE

...and some reasons to learn it

Around the world, four hundred eighty three million (483) people speaks spanish like native lenguage and nine million (9) speak it as second. As you know, in América Latina, from México to the south of Argentina’s Patagonia, the spanish is the official language in twenty three (23) countries, and in Spain and Equatorial Guinea, in Africa also. But this language goes trough the borders occident world: after the english and french, is the third lenguage theaching in the world, but in the future, will be the first. Why we are waiting for that moment?

Learn spanish is learn the America Latina and Spain’s history. No one can understand the daily language used if don’t understand the culture and the significates. The type of people in America Latina is crossbreed (here we say ‘mestizo’), no half-blood -in that case we are a double-blood-. So all the citizens across the geography and space, all with many identities (italian, from Spain, China, Corea, african continent, Asia, North europa and indigenous society), talk with others trough this language.

In this sense, the pluricultural people have many influences: politics, cultural, but also artistic, from the literature, music and the cinema. So, learning spanish lenguage as communication tools is aprehend at the same time the social meanings and the artistic expretions diversity of America Latina, region in which a lot of artists were born, writers like Juan Rulfo (México), Gabriel García Márquez (Colombia), Jorge Luis Borges or José Ingenieros (Argentina) and musicians like Victor Jara and Pablo Neruda (Chile), Carlos Gardel (Argentina) and Silvio Rodríguez (Cuba).
